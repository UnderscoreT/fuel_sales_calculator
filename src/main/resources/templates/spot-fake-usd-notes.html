How to Spot Fake USD Notes: Quick Attendant Guide

Last updated: August 10, 2025 • 7–9 min read

Counterfeit detection starts with three moves: touch, tilt, and light. Build the habit and you’ll spot most fakes in seconds—even during rush hour.

1) Touch: Feel the Paper & Raised Print

  • Feel the paper: Genuine USD uses a cotton-linen blend; it should feel firm, not glossy or waxy.
  • Raised ink: Rub fingertips across “United States of America,” portrait shoulder, and denomination numerals; genuine notes have texture.

2) Tilt: Color-Shifting Ink & Security Thread

  • Color-shift numerals: On newer notes (e.g., $10/$20/$50/$100), the bottom-right numeral shifts color when tilted.
  • Security thread: A thin embedded strip that glows under UV and reads the denomination; position varies by note.

3) Light: Watermark & Microprinting

  • Watermark: Hold to light; a faint portrait or numeral should be visible from both sides, not printed on the surface.
  • Microprinting: Tiny text around the portrait/lines; fakes blur here. Use a simple loupe if available.

4) Fast Counterfeit Tells

  • Edges cut unevenly; serial numbers misaligned or identical on multiple notes.
  • Ink that smears easily; flat, glossy print without raised texture.
  • Missing or printed-on “thread” instead of embedded.

5) What To Do If You Suspect a Fake

  1. Stay calm and polite; do not return the note if rules require holding it.
  2. Inform your supervisor and follow station policy (incident log, CCTV bookmark).
  3. Note the customer description politely if appropriate; prioritize safety.
