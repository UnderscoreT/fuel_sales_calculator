Fuel Station Safety Tips for Attendants

Last updated: August 10, 2025 • 6–8 min read

Working around flammable liquids requires calm routines, good communication, and habits that prevent small issues from becoming emergencies. Use this checklist during every shift.

1) Daily Safety Routine (Start of Shift)

  • PPE check: Closed shoes, no loose clothing, hair secured. Avoid synthetic gloves that can build static.
  • Fire extinguishers: Confirm correct class (ABC/BC), gauge in the green, easy access (unblocked), inspection tag up-to-date.
  • Emergency stops: Know the emergency shut-off and who can authorize use.
  • Spill kit: Granules/absorbent, pads, disposal bag, and signage ready.

2) Static, Sparks & No-Phone Zone

  • Ask customers to switch off engines and avoid smoking/open flames.
  • Discourage phone use near pumps; phones are also distractions when handling nozzles.
  • Touch a grounded metal surface before fueling to discharge static; avoid synthetic garments that spark.

3) Clean, Contain, Communicate: Spill Response

  1. Stop & isolate: Stop fueling, keep people away, switch off ignition sources.
  2. Contain: Use absorbent to circle and pull fuel inward; never hose into drains.
  3. Notify: Inform your supervisor, log the incident, and dispose of waste per station rules.

4) Card, Coupon & Cash Handling

Counting mistakes create tension and delays. Use Cash Breakdown Maker to balance till cash fast and reduce queue times after a spill or rush hour.

5) Customer Safety Reminders

  • Politely request engine off and no smoking; point to signage if needed.
  • Insist on approved containers placed on the ground before filling.
  • Stop fueling if unsafe behavior continues—safety rules protect everyone.

6) End-of-Shift: Accurate Records

Accurate logs help detect leaks, shrinkage, or meter faults early. Use the SizaFuel calculator to compute totals from start/end readings and track coupon/card litres.

Quick Reference

  • Know extinguishers + shut-off.
  • Control static and ignition sources.
  • Contain spills—never wash away.
