Customer Service Skills for Fuel Attendants

Last updated: August 10, 2025 • 6–8 min read

Quick Scripts

  • Greeting: “Morning! Which product and how much today?”
  • Safety reminder: “Please switch off the engine for me. Thank you.”
  • Complaint: “Thanks for raising that—I’ll check the meter and get my supervisor if needed.”

Accuracy Under Pressure

Use SizaFuel to record readings and Cash Breakdown Maker for fast banking totals—less head-down counting, more customer attention.

Queue Management

  • Point customers to free nozzles; coordinate with colleagues when queues build.
  • Move complex cases (fleet cards, coupon disputes) to one side to keep flow.

Handling Difficult Situations

  • Stay calm, state facts, and refer to posted rules for consistency.
  • Involve a supervisor early for payment disputes.

Build Loyalty

  • Remember regulars’ preferences (fuel type, amounts).
  • Offer a quick “Thanks—drive safe!” to end on a positive note.
